Chole Bhature

Chole Bhature is a mouthwatering and popular North Indian dish that effortlessly captures the essence of Indian cuisine. This tempting dish consists of spicy and flavorful chickpeas, known as "chole," served alongside soft and fluffy deep-fried bread called "bhature." The combination of robust spices, tangy flavors, and the contrasting textures of the chickpeas and the puffed bread make Chole Bhature an irresistible treat for the taste buds. Whether enjoyed for breakfast, lunch, or dinner, this iconic dish is a celebration of the rich culinary diversity that India has to offer.

    Ingridients:-

  • 1 cup dried chickpeas (soaked overnight)
  • 2 medium-sized onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 2 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon amchur (dry mango powder)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • Cooking oil
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup semolina (suji)
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (as needed)

    Instructions:-

  1. Cooking Chickpeas (Chole):
  2. In a pressure cooker, add soaked chickpeas, water, and a pinch of salt. Cook until chickpeas are tender.
  3. In a separate pan, heat oil and add cumin seeds. Once they splutter, add chopped onions and green chilies. Saute until onions are golden brown.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and saut√© until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Stir in tomato puree and cook until the oil separates from the mixture.
  6. Add coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, amchur, and salt. Mix well.
  7. Add cooked chickpeas to the spice mixture. Simmer on low heat for 15-20 minutes.
  8. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  9. Preparing Bhature:
  10. In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, semolina, yogurt, sugar, baking soda, and salt.
  11. Gradually add water and knead the mixture into a soft and smooth dough.
  12. Cover the dough and let it rest for 2-3 hours.
  13. Divide the dough into small balls and roll them out into oval-shaped discs.
  14. Heat oil in a deep frying pan. Once hot, gently slide in the rolled bhature and fry until they puff up and turn golden brown.
  15. Remove excess oil by placing the bhature on absorbent paper.
  16. Serving:
  17. Serve the hot and spicy chole alongside the puffed bhature.
  18. Garnish with chopped onions, green chilies, and a sprinkle of fresh coriander leaves.
  19. Accompany with yogurt or pickles for a delightful and satisfying meal.
